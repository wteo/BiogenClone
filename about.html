The Science Behind Red Light Therapy

Red light therapy (RLT), also known as low-level laser therapy (LLLT) or photobiomodulation, is a treatment that uses low-wavelength red light to improve the function of mitochondria in your cells.

When delivered at the correct wavelengths and energy levels, red and near-infrared light can penetrate the skin and underlying tissues, where it stimulates cellular energy production and enhances the body's natural healing mechanisms.

Key Scientific Principles:

  • Mitochondrial Enhancement: Red light therapy enhances ATP production, the energy currency of your cells
  • Increased Circulation: Promotes better blood flow and tissue oxygenation
  • Reduced Oxidative Stress: Helps neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation
  • Collagen Production: Stimulates fibroblasts to produce more collagen, improving skin health
