Mitochondria

The cellular powerhouses that drive your health and performance

The Powerhouses of Your Cells

Mitochondria are the "power plants" of the cell—they make energy in the form of ATP from food, oxygen and water. Our protocol enhances mitochondrial function—and therefore cellular energy production—in two ways:

1

Tailored Supplementation

A specifically tailored supplement program that supports the biochemical needs of mitochondria.

2

Red Light Therapy

Red light therapy, which has been shown to stimulate complex IV of the electron transport chain, generating more ATP and reducing oxidative stress on mitochondria.

Why Mitochondrial Health Matters

Energy Production

Mitochondria produce over 90% of the energy your body needs to function. They convert nutrients from food into ATP, the molecule that powers cellular activities. Healthy mitochondria mean more energy for everything you do.

Athletic Performance

Athletes rely heavily on mitochondrial function. Greater mitochondrial density and efficiency directly translate to improved endurance, strength, and recovery capacity.

Cognitive Function

Your brain is highly energy-dependent. Optimal mitochondrial function supports cognitive performance, mental clarity, and may help prevent neurodegenerative conditions.

Immune Health

Immune cells need abundant energy to fight infections. Well-functioning mitochondria support your immune system's ability to respond effectively to challenges.

Longevity

Mitochondrial dysfunction is associated with aging and age-related diseases. Supporting mitochondrial health may slow age-related decline and promote longevity.

Recovery & Sleep

Efficient mitochondria help your body recover from stress and physical exertion. They also play a role in regulating sleep cycles and ensuring restorative sleep.

How Red Light Therapy Enhances Mitochondrial Function

Red and near-infrared light therapy works directly on your mitochondria through several key mechanisms:

Cytochrome C Oxidase Activation

Red light is absorbed by cytochrome c oxidase (Complex IV) in the mitochondrial electron transport chain. This absorption enhances the activity of this crucial enzyme, accelerating the production of ATP (cellular energy).

Nitric Oxide Displacement

Red light therapy displaces nitric oxide (NO) from cytochrome c oxidase. NO typically inhibits this enzyme, so its displacement allows for increased oxygen consumption and ATP production.

Reduced Oxidative Stress

Photobiomodulation reduces the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) while increasing antioxidant defenses, protecting mitochondria from oxidative damage.

Increased Mitochondrial Biogenesis

Regular red light therapy stimulates the creation of new mitochondria within cells, increasing overall energy production capacity.
